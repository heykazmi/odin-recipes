Omurice

What is Omurice?

Omurice, pronounced "omu-raisu" in Japanese, translates to "omelette rice" and is a delightful fusion of Western-style omelette and Japanese fried rice.

How to make Omurice?

Get ready to whip up a delightful Omurice that's straight out of your favorite anime! Here's a guide to transform this classic dish into a vibrant, character-themed masterpiece:

So simple yet tasty, every anime fan loves to have a bit of it, lets see how its done


Ingredients:

For the fried rice:

  • 2 cups cooked rice
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • 1/2 onion, diced
  • 1/2 cup chopped vegetables (carrots, peas, mushrooms, etc.)
  • 1/4 cup cooked chicken or ham, shredded (optional)
  • 2 eggs, beaten
  • 2 tablespoons ketchup
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • Salt and pepper to taste

For the omelette:

  • 3 eggs
  • 2 tablespoons milk
  • 1 tablespoon butter
  • Ketchup or demi-glace for decorating

Steps:

  1. Prepare the fried rice: Heat oil in a pan and sauté onion until softened. Add vegetables and cook until crisp-tender. Stir in cooked rice, chicken/ham (if using), ketchup, soy sauce, salt, and pepper. Cook for a few minutes, ensuring everything is heated through.
  2. Cook the omelette: In a separate bowl, whisk eggs and milk. Heat butter in a pan over medium heat. Pour in the egg mixture and tilt the pan to spread it evenly. Let the bottom set slightly before gently pushing cooked edges towards the center. Repeat until the omelette is almost cooked through but still slightly runny.
  3. Assemble the Omurice: Place a mound of fried rice in the center of the omelette. Carefully fold the sides over the rice, creating a half-moon shape. Use a spatula to gently transfer the Omurice to a plate.

Tips:

  • a non-stick pan for easy omelette flipping.
  • Don't overcook the omelette, it should be slightly runny for the best texture.
  • Get creative with your decorations! The possibilities are endless.
  • Most importantly, have fun and enjoy the process!
