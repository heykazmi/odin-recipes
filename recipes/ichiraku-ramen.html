Ichiraku Ramen

What is Ichiraku Ramen?

Ichiraku Ramen Ramen is a chain of Ramen selling Restaurants. It is owned by Teuchi Ichiraku Ramen who started out with a single small shop in Konoha. Ichiraku Ramen Ramen is the favourite Shop of Naruto Uzumaki.

How to make Ichiraku Ramen?

Get ready to whip up a delightful Ichiraku Ramen that's straight out of your favorite anime! Here's a guide to make this dish!

So simple yet tasty, every anime fan loves to have a bit of it, lets see how its done


Ingredients:

Broth:

  • 4-5 cups good quality chicken stock (preferably low-sodium)
  • 4 spring onions/scallions, trimmed and cut into 3-inch pieces or just in half
  • 4-5 cloves garlic, whole cloves are fine
  • 2-inch piece ginger, sliced
  • 4 fresh red chili peppers or 1 tbsp. dried chili flakes (crushed red pepper) (optional)
  • ¼ cup light soy sauce
  • ¼ cup mirin
  • 8 oz. fresh shiitake mushrooms, stems trimmed and sliced, or cut in half (optional)
  • 6 pieces dried shiitake mushrooms (optional)
  • 12-13 oz. dried ramen noodles or 4 portions (or 4 portions of fresh ramen noodles)

Toppings:

  • 2 soft-boiled eggs
  • 1/2 cup sliced scallions
  • 1 cup shredded cooked chicken
  • Sesame seeds
  • Chili oil or Sriracha

Steps:

  1. Make the broth: Heat sesame oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add ginger and garlic, cook for 30 seconds. Pour in chicken stock and water, bring to a boil. Reduce heat to low and simmer for 10 minutes.
  2. Add miso paste: Remove pot from heat, stir in miso paste until dissolved. Add soy sauce, mirin, and sriracha (optional). Return to heat and simmer for 5 minutes more.
  3. Prepare the toppings: Cook ramen noodles according to package instructions. Soft-boil an egg (optional) and slice. Shred cooked chicken. Crumble nori seaweed (optional).
  4. Assemble the ramen: Divide noodles among bowls. Pour in the hot broth, leaving room for toppings. Add your preferred toppings: sliced chicken, soft-boiled egg (cut in half), scallions, nori seaweed (optional), and sesame seeds. Enjoy your spicy miso ramen!

Tips:

  • a non-stick pan for easy omelette flipping.
  • Don't overcook the omelette, it should be slightly runny for the best texture.
  • Get creative with your decorations! The possibilities are endless.
  • Most importantly, have fun and enjoy the process!
